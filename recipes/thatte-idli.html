Thatte Idli

Description

Thatte Idli is a Karnataka-originating South Indian breakfast staple, distinct for its large, flat, plate-like shape derived from its name “Thatte” (Kannada for "plate").

It's a thicker, more voluminous version of traditional idli, steamed in large, round molds on special plates. Made from fermented rice and urad dal batter, it's served with coconut chutney and sambar

Ingredients

  • Rice – 1 cup
  • Urad dal – 1/4 cup
  • Flattened rice (poha) – 2 tbsp
  • Salt – to taste

Steps

  1. Soak rice, urad dal, and poha together for 4-6 hours, then grind into a smooth, thick batter.
  2. Allow the batter to ferment in a warm place overnight or for about 8-10 hours.
  3. Add salt and gently mix the batter without deflating it.
  4. Pour into greased thatte idli plates and steam for 15-20 minutes until soft and cooked through.
